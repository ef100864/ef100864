Cargo Planes

Cargo planes are designed to transport goods and materials. They are often operated by shipping companies like DHL and FedEx. Unlike passenger planes, cargo planes typically have high-mounted wings to optimize long-distance flight and cargo space. The largest cargo plane ever built was the Antonov An-225 Mriya, with a wingspan of 88.4 meters, wider than a football field.

Passenger Planes

Passenger planes are used to transport people and are equipped with features to ensure safety and comfort. Operated by airlines, these planes often have low-mounted wings for stability and efficiency. The largest passenger plane is the Airbus A380, capable of carrying up to 853 passengers.

Military Planes

Military planes are used by air forces for defense and combat missions. Their designs, often with mid-mounted wings, prioritize speed and maneuverability. Pilots require specialized training to operate these aircraft due to their advanced systems and weaponry. The fastest military plane is the Lockheed Martin X-15A-2, reaching speeds of Mach 6.7.
