Different Plane Manufacturers

Airbus

Airbus is the world's third-largest plane manufacturer. This French-German company has produced 15,500 planes over its 54-year history. Airbus is known for creating the world's largest passenger airplane, the Airbus A380, and the eco-friendly Airbus Beluga. The company has a rich history and ambitious plans, including developing the world's first eco-friendly military helicopter. Visit the official Airbus website for more details:

Airbus Website

Boeing

Boeing is the world's largest and oldest aircraft manufacturer, having produced 20,000 planes. It holds records for being the largest aircraft producer and creating significant military transport planes. Beyond aviation, Boeing manufactures satellites and has contributed parts to the Mars Rover and the first rocket to land on the moon. Explore more on the official Boeing website:

Boeing Website

Antonov

Antonov, a Ukrainian manufacturer founded in 1938 by the Soviet government, is renowned as the world's best cargo plane manufacturer. It holds records for the largest plane, the largest cargo plane, and the highest-altitude cargo plane. Antonov operates its own transport company and retains most of its planes. Notably, a plane it created is still used by NASA, despite being purchased in 2008. Learn more on Antonov's website:
